Immediate response

Phone calls will be taken immediately by Victim Support, who operate 24 hours a day, every day of the year.

Teams from Victim Support, British Red Cross and Samaritans will communicate using the existing WhatsApp group.

Decision making

Key decisions that are made when an emergency starts:

  • Should the service be promoted?
  • How the service should be promoted?
  • When should the servide be promoted?
  • Which organisation will answer the calls?
  • When to scale down the service?

Decision will be informed by:

  • Scale of the emergency
  • Anticipated and on-going need
  • Media coverage
  • Number of calls recieved

Which organisation takes calls

Who takes the call will depend on the type of emergency.

Crime or suspected crime emergencies

If the emergency is a crime or suspect Victim Support will continue to take calls from people.

Victim Support will signpost or warm transfer callers to British Red Cross and Samaritans as necessary.

Non-crime emergencies

British Red Cross will takes call in non-crime emerencies. For examples natural diasters like flooding.

Victim Support will take calls in the first few hours. Then at a agreed time calls will be diverted to British Red Cross. This allows time for British Red Cross to put staff and equipment in place.

British Red Cross will signpost or warm transfer callers to Victim and Samaritans as necessary.

Promoting the service

Content to be added following conversations between communications teams from Victim Support and British Red Cross. This is likely to include:

  • What channels to promote the serivce through
  • Templates for partners to consistently promote the service
  • Internal communications

Working with National Emergencies Trust

Content to be added following conversations between National Emergencies Trust and British Red Cross. For example the process to refer people to access.

Reporting service

Reporting templates to be agreed between between Victim Support and British Red Cross. This is likely to include:

  • Number of people calling
  • Types of support people call about
  • Unmet needs
  • Issues with the service

Scaling up the service

There will be times when service capacity will need to be increased, for example:

  • The organisation taking calls doesn't have enough staff available to take the number of calls
  • Another emergency starts
  • A large proportion of calls are from people wanting to access funds or donate them

In these situations a decision can be made to divert calls between Victim Support and British Red Cross using a dual option answering system.

Out-of-hours and scaling down the service

Victim Support will take calls out-of-hours, for example when British Red Cross is unable to staff the service during this time or demand for the service is minimal.

Calls will be diverted back to Victim Support at an agree time, upon assessing the on-going:

  • Number of people calling
  • Types of support people call about
  • Unmet needs

During this time, a process will be put in place to refer people to support best given by British Red Cross or Samaritans.
