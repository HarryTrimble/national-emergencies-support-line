Deciding to promote the support line in a major emergency

The support line is available 24 hours a day, and calls will always be answered. Its designed for people affected by major emergencies, so it will only be actively promoted during these times.

Examples of previous major emergencies include Manchester Arena attack and Grenfell tower fire.

The decision of whether or not to promote the support line lies with Victim Support and British Red Cross. Other agencies can request for the support line to be promoted.

Decision to promote the support line will be judged by:

  • Scale of the emergency
  • Anticipated, on-going and unmet need
  • Number of calls recieved
  • Level of demand on local agencies
  • Whether other support line are open
  • Media coverage

The decisions need to be made will be:

  • Should the support line be promoted?
  • Which organisation will answer the calls?
  • How the support line should be promoted?
  • When should the support line be promoted?
  • When to scale down the support line?

Key decisions about the support line are made by:

  • Chief Executive, Victim Support
  • Head of Crisis Response, British Red Cross

A select group of representatives from Victim Support, British Red Cross and Samaritans communicate using a National Emergencies Support Line WhatsApp group.
